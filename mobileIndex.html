As part of a school trip to New York City, I was once on the trading floor of a multinational bank. I was surprised by what I heard. The floor was quieter than most university libraries. The class was partnered with those working there. Most students were paired with traders, but I was fortunate to talk to a programmer. He said something to me that has stuck with me since. “My job is to put these traders out of work.” I was witnessing the effects of technology firsthand. He mentioned some people left with their things packed in tears, while others simply understood that this was the new norm. Since that day, I have developed a passion for technology, learning languages, such as Python, JavaScript, and SQL. Now, each day I work to fuse the fields of technology and finance.
